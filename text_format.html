History - Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630-1680) was a Maratha warrior king and founder of the Maratha Empire in India. He is revered for his military prowess, innovative strategies, and his contribution to the decline of Mughal rule. Shivaji was born in Shivneri Fort, Maharashtra, to Bhosale and Jijabai . He was raised under the guidance of his mother and Brahmin Dadaji Konda-dev, who instilled in him a strong sense of justice and leadership.

Born on February 19, 1630, in the Fort near Pune , Maharashtra, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj is considered one of the greatest warriors and a true visionary leader in Indian history. Shivaji Maharaj’s biography epitomizes courage, progressive rule, and a just society for people. Known for his strategic brilliance, courageous leadership, and unwavering commitment to people’s welfare, Shivaji Maharaj has left a legacy that inspires people even today.
